First we need to talk about the null hypothesis. The null hypothesis is a state of non-belief. In science, as well as anything but history and religion, it's assumed correct. It's difficult-to-impossible to disprove the existance of anything. If I told you that there's a small black teapot orbiting the sun, slightly between the orbits of earth and mars, you wouldn't believe me. This is true for most, if not all claims. For the claim to be accepted, there has to be evidence. Not direct observation, but evidence. If I say that my pen will fall if I drop it, that's a claim. My evidence for this is me dropping the pen and it falling. If I say that my pen will shoot into the sun if I click it, that's an equally valid claim as the first. But the fact that I can't provide any evidence for it makes it invalid.

You're likely already seeing how this applies to the claims made by religion. "But what about the bible, the infallible word of god? Isn't that evidence enough?" you might say. Evidence? No. Infalliable? Well, there's a LOT of problems with the bible. Oh boy, there's a lot of problems with the bible. Strap in.

Common Christian Arguments

Okay, so the bible is false, that still doesn't disprove God.

Okay, fine. I'll give you that. We can't disprove Christianity. But what we can do is show that there's no actual evidence for it. Remember the null hypothesis from earlier? This is when it comes into play. With nothing to actually back up your claim, it's just as valid as any other religion, or any other claim I can make about anything.

But what about the apostles, didn't they die for Christianity?

That says nothing about how true it is. There have been people who die for Islam, for Hinduism, and for every other religion. Are their religions validated the same way that yours are?

Why not just believe? If you're wrong, you lose nothing, but if you're right you gain so much?

That's already been thought of. Otherwise known as Pascal's wager, this theory rings true only if you take only two positions, Christianity and Atheism. If you think of Hinduism, or Islam, or any other religion, suddenly your odds drop dramatically. And if you take into account any other imaginary gods, like, for instance, the Anti-Christian god, who punishes you if you do things that make the Christian god happy, and vice versa, you end up with a net gain of 0. Thus, it doesn't matter what you believe in, mathematically there's no afterlife.

But what created the universe? Every effect has a cause, right?

Says who? Science so far thinks the universe has always existed, in some form or another. Also, why is your god exempt? What caused it?

You just gotta have faith.
