  2. Reading Comprehension is very effective. The book metaphor really lends itself greatly to how her picture is stuck in a chapter of the author's life, marking a chapter she could never finish, or basically saying how she could never get past this event, something about this girl she could never get over. Being as short as it is makes the reader want more, but they never get it.
  3. This piece shows greatly how a disease can impact people. It shows the struggles of not only the person suffering but the people around them. The title shows how suddenly it happens, how disease can strike without warning. ...but he was healthy is something often said when a disease strikes suddenly.
  4. It's about someone with a traumatic past. Something she can't get over. When the author talks about the untimely death of her childhood it makes me think about something that happened to her.
